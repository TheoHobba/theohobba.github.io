Personal Project: Popular Astronomy Posts

A lot of people use social media nowadays. Of the many social medias, Instagram specializes in focusing on the use of visual media. Since I enjoy (astro)photography, I decided to use Instagram where I can use my (astro)photos as stocks for personalized popular astronomy posts.

I decided to cover topics not frequently discussed in other similarly-themed posts. The goal is to bring up niche information about astronomy to public. I base the topics by highlighting interesting contents (objects, phenomena, etc.) captured in my (astro)photos.

As of now, my main target audience are Indonesians. Hence, my posts are currently written in Indonesian.

Ditara.id Posts

I have experience working with my colleagues as a content writer and editor for their popular astronomy account, Ditara.id. My colleagues launched this account to focus on popularizing astronomy through art and technology, one of which is in the form of posts and reels that appeal to Indonesians. As a content writer, I wrote about topics that offer opoprtunities for readers to get in touch with astronomy, such as delivering basic astronomy concepts and connecting astronomy with human culture. As an editor, I helped writers refine their contents to ensure balance between content complexity and comprehensibility, content clarity, and delivery tone.

Fenomena Tidal Locking

Is it true that the Moon has near and far sides? Yes, we always see its side that faces us (near side) and not the other (far side). The cause is tidal locking, a phenomenon known to exist in other celestial bodies.
